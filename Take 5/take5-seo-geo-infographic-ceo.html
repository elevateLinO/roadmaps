Take5.com Rebuild · Driven Brands
Strengthening discovery & citation in search & AI:
SEO, AEO & GEO
Layered structural improvements so the work you’re already doing in SEO, content, and local can compound -> earning deeper discovery and more trusted citations over time.
Five structural priorities where the rebuild adds the most lift.
1
Machine-readable depth can still go further
High impact
Today
Schema and feeds are a baseline; a fuller entity graph, AI-ready index, and server-rendered critical facts would help crawlers use every signal you publish.
After rebuild
Unified Schema, a canonical AI feed, SSR for critical facts, and consistent page structure.
2
Location data is fragmented and slow to fix
High impact
Today
NAP and Google Business Profiles drift; emergency fixes take days; local pages carry weak “local” signals.
After rebuild
One source of truth, fast overrides, auto-synced listings, stronger local pages — wait times in later phases.
3
Content doesn’t answer searchable questions
High impact
Today
Thin service copy, unstructured articles, and voice rules that avoid the terms people actually search.
After rebuild
Services hub, FAQ, and answer-first modules — editorial guidelines aligned with intent, not just brand tone.
4
Headroom to grow in AI-assisted discovery
High impact
Today
Stronger brand and entity signals, plus a fuller off-site footprint, would help close the gap vs. competitors on conversational and “near me” queries.
After rebuild
Defined brand entity, structured off-site program, conversational-ready local content, and a prompt audit to steer priorities.
5
We can’t prove what works, especially in AI
Medium impact
Today
Analytics don’t line up; no experimentation loop; no view of AI citations or unsampled conversion paths.
After rebuild
Shared data layer, A/B testing, Share of Model tracking, and BigQuery for full-funnel reporting.
Rankings depend on many signals; this work extends structural runway so SEO, content, and listings can compound.
